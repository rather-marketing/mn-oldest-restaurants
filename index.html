A List of Minnesota's Oldest Restaurants

Throughout time, many restaurants in Minnesota have faced periods of turmoil that they couldn't withstand - fires, the Great Depression, Prohibition, and Covid. Read on for a list of Minnesota's oldest restaurants that have stood the test of time and are still operating today.

Hubbell House in Mantorville - Est. 1854

Back in 1854 when the Hubbell Hotel was built, there was a single dining room. The restaurant was expanded when the Pappas family assumed control of the restaurant in 1946 and to this day, continue to follow the family motto:

"Put a good meal on the plate, charge a fair price, offer a pleasant atmosphere and treat your customers and employees right. If you do, people will come back.”

Neumann’s Bar & Grill in North St. Paul - Est. 1887

This establishment makes the list with one caveat - it opened in 1887 as a saloon with no mention of serving food. Fortunately, the building and character have remained mostly unchanged. You can still see the keyhole window to the upstairs prohibition-era speakeasy that ensured only "welcomed" guests could enter.

Monte Carlo in Minneapolis - Est. 1906

That it is so old seems a secret. This establishment features a copper-topped bar, intimate booths in the main dining room, an expansive patio and private dining rooms.

Pickwick Restaurant & Pub in Duluth - Est. 1914

The Pickwick opened in 1888 as the Old Saloon within Fitger's, but it has operated at its current location since 1914, surviving the Great Depression and changing ownership only a few times. The Pickwick offers award-worthy meals with an Old World atmosphere.

Deluxe Coney Island in Duluth - Est. 1928

With no alcohol on the menu, this establishment is a true contender that dates back to pre-prohibition times and is still family owned. They offer coneys & gyros served with their signature Coney Sauce crafted from the same old-world family recipe.

Fires & Covid

A few restaurants are frequently mentioned among the oldest in the state but did not make the above list for 2024. Bar Harbor Supper Club in Nisswa - burned to the ground and rebuilt, then fully renovated. Gluek’s Bar & Restaurant in Minneapolis - lost to fire in 1989, then fully rebuilt. Grant House Hotel in Rush City - Its current restaurant, The Fort, is a new tenant as of January 2024. Mickey’s Diner dining car - currently closed, possibly permanently. Pracna On Main - currently closed, possibly permanently.
